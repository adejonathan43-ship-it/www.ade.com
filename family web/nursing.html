Research Project

Knowledge, Practice and Acceptance of Family Planning Among Women of Child Bearing Age

Ogugu Community, Akoko-Edo Local Government Area, Edo State

CHAPTER ONE: INTRODUCTION

Background to the Study

Family planning refers to the deliberate control of the number and spacing of children through the use of contraceptive methods. According to the World Health Organization (2024), family planning enables individuals and couples to determine the number and timing of their children.

Family planning plays a vital role in improving maternal and child health, reducing maternal mortality, and preventing unintended pregnancies. In many developing countries including Nigeria, family planning services are promoted as part of reproductive health programs.

Statement of the Problem

Despite increasing awareness of family planning services, many women in rural communities still experience challenges in accessing and using contraceptive methods. Cultural beliefs, fear of side effects, and lack of adequate health education often influence acceptance.

Research Questions

  • What is the level of knowledge of family planning among women?
  • What family planning methods are practiced?
  • What factors influence acceptance of family planning?

CHAPTER TWO: LITERATURE REVIEW

Concept of Family Planning

Family planning involves the use of contraceptive methods to control the number and spacing of children in a family. It helps improve the health of mothers and children while promoting economic stability within households.

Types of Family Planning Methods

Modern Methods

  • Oral contraceptive pills
  • Injectable contraceptives
  • Implants
  • Intrauterine Devices (IUD)
  • Male condoms
  • Female condoms
  • Dental dams
  • Sterilization

Traditional Methods

  • Withdrawal method
  • Calendar/Rhythm method
  • Lactational Amenorrhea Method

Emergency Contraception

Emergency contraception is used after unprotected intercourse to prevent pregnancy. Methods include emergency contraceptive pills and copper intrauterine devices.

CHAPTER THREE: RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

Research Design

The study adopted a descriptive survey research design to investigate knowledge and use of family planning methods among women in Ogugu Community.

Population of the Study

The population consisted of women of childbearing age (15–49 years) living in Ogugu Community in Akoko-Edo Local Government Area.

Sampling Technique

Simple random sampling was used to select respondents for the study.

Instrument for Data Collection

A structured questionnaire was used to collect primary data from respondents.

CHAPTER FIVE: SUMMARY, CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS

Summary

The study assessed knowledge, practice and acceptance of family planning among women of childbearing age in Ogugu Community.

Conclusion

Although awareness of family planning is relatively high, utilization remains limited due to cultural and social factors.

Recommendations

  • Improve community health education programs.
  • Increase access to reproductive health services.
  • Encourage male partner participation in family planning.
